Racking Structure and Modules Installation:
1. Racking:  
Subcontractor is responsible for providing all labor, equipment, fuel and material to receive deliveries, take inventory, distribute, install and perform the work listed below in accordance with the Contract Documents so that the system is completely functional and operational.
2. Racking Summary: 
Proper means and methods for work performed under this Scope of Work must be used to ensure that racking is installed in the correct locations, square and true per the Valmont Manual and Drawings specifications.  Racking and all Racking components to be installed in the correct locations with consideration to alerting site supervisor of layout problems before installing. Subcontractor shall Torque all fasteners per the Contract Drawings and Specifications. Work includes all necessary adjustments for the aesthetic approval of the Prime Contractor and ESS. Extra care shall be given to the quality of the work.
Subcontractor Shall provide all necessary tools for the installation of work. (1) One Reach Lift (Lull) or similar 4x4 8k shall be rented for the receipt, delivery, distribution and installation of the Racking system.  
3. Racking and Post Distribution: 
The Subcontractor is responsible to provide Equipment and Manpower for the distribution of the racking to the location of install.  1 Reach Lift 4x4 8k shall be rented for Racking during racking installation and distribution. This subcontractor to receive and distribute all posts. Posts are to be installed by others.
4. Module Installation:
The Subcontractor is responsible for notifying Contractor immediately, before or during offloading, of materials damaged in shipping or offloading.
Subcontractor to provide all equipment and manpower required to distribute and install Modules, including, but not limited to the following:
Installation of modules to meet racking and module manufacturer tolerances per module vendor installation manual and the Contract Document requirements.
Special consideration is required by subcontractor to install modules using the specifications provided by module vendor and contract documents to account for grade changes and provide array rows that are gradual in angular changes and of flow like appearance. Extra care shall be used in the handling, distributing, moving and installing of all modules and replacement shall be at the cost of Subcontractor for modules broken during installation. 
Subcontractor shall provide proper Electrical Licensing attached to the (Modules installation work) so as to be (or to be) in compliance with all local codes and regulations. Two (2) Skidsteers shall be rented during Module Installation Work For the receipt, delivery, distribution and installation of Modules.

Electrical

Install DC wire, connectors, termination, testing, grounding/bonding at array. Install tracker controllers and wiring to motors, termination.  
1.       Plug together module whips to create strings of 28 (28 modules per string). 
a.       Pre-configured jumpers will be provided. 
b.       (Tracker tables consist of 28 modules with approx. 11” bearing gaps at each 7 modules. Jumpers will be 
           installed at these gaps).
2.       Install string wire from module string connection to inverter, routed along rack and through CAB system.  
a.       #10 PV wire (red = positive, black = negative)
b.       Follow electrical drawings for string, row, and inverter location/identification
3.       Set (CAB Products) wire hangers on “AC/DC CAB system” messenger wire at designated intervals for wire support. 
4.       Label each conductor at each end/termination point.
a.       UV/weather rated labels required are to be provided by Contractor and installed by Subcontractor. 
b.       Follow drawings for numbering method 
5.       Wire management with zip ties. Approx. qty-2 per module min. 
a.       Wire management mockup with the help of Contractor will be required upon commencing task. Approval of 
          means and method/mockup is required prior to completing more than 2/3 racks.
b.       Module whip and string wire to be routed along rack/back of module to stay 16” from Valmont tracker 
           control wiring.
6.       Install Split loom on wire drip loops/transition loops from rack to rack and rack to CAB system.
a.       Wire drip loops/transition loops will be secured with wire ties where applicable
b.       Loops to be per Valmont manual, secured and installed to prevent tension and/or abrasion on wire due to 
 	           tracker range of motion or sharp edges.
7.       Install bonding jumper between start and stop posts at table breaks where North/South tables do not share the 
           same post.  
a.       #6 copper with ground lug installed on each post. 
b.       Anit-Oxidant compound to be applied to all ground bond points 
8.       Install ground bond from last post of tracker row and bond to continuous ground along CAB run.
a.       #6 copper wire with ground lug on post and bond to CAB run #6 ground with ‘C’ Crimp.
b.       To be routed with DC wire drip loop from Tracker to CAB system.
9.       Install #6 copper ground (solid bare) wire routed in CAB hanger system 
a.       To run continuous along full-length of CAB run.
10.   Install #6 ground wire bond from continuous CAB ground to each inverter.
11.   Install MC-4 connectors at each end of string conductors. 
a.       Strings shall not be connected to modules or inverter till testing verified complete by ESS.
12.   Intentionally left blank.
13.   After Contractor performs VOC test, Subcontractor to plug strings into inverters. 

Tacker Control Wiring (in array)
1.       Install SKC tracker controllers.
a.       Mount to Valmont end pier on per drilled holes in pier (a few piers may require mounting holes to be drilled)
2.       Install actuator/motor control wiring from SKC controller to each motor.
a.       To be routed along “Valmont CAB system” and along tracker row to reach each motor. 
b.       Wire management with wire ties as applicable
c.       Wire management mock-up approval required 
3.       Set wire hangers on “Valmont CAB system” messenger wire at designated intervals for wire support. 
4.       Terminate control wiring at motor
5.       Terminate control wiring at SKC controller
6.       Install #6 copper (bare solid) ground along “Valmont CAB system” 
a.       Bond continuous ground to each Valmont pier with #6 copper jumper, ‘C’ crimp and lay-in lug at pier
7.       Verify controller wiring function at time of system commissioning.
