# Operator-Dashboard Upgrade Slate — 2026-07-19

## Provenance

Produced the day the `/system` map + DASH-12/13 verbs landed (#614), in answer to the
operator's "what depth / design / documentation / capability can we add" question. Method:
a 5-agent survey — a code-grounded feasibility sweep of 20 candidates (every verdict cites
file:line against live HEAD), three independent lenses (operator-ops mined from session
logs; successor-operator mined from the §43/§44 docs + the troubleshooting tree's 50
failure modes; incident-replay mined from past outages), and a judge pass that merged 54
raw candidates into the tiered slate below. Feasibility verdicts are the grounding
agent's, not the lenses'; anything a lens proposed without grounding is marked UNVERIFIED.

**How to use this doc:** pick a tier-1 slice (the judge's sequencing note at the bottom
recommends the first build), confirm any UNVERIFIED data claims before building tier-2
items 1-3, and treat the REJECTED section as settled doctrine posture — those stay dead
unless Seth explicitly reopens one.

54 raw candidates (C1-C20 grounding; 11 operator-ops + 4 pre-rejected; 10 successor; 9 incident-replay) merge to 33 slate entries + 6 rejections. Feasibility verdicts are quoted from the grounding report only; lens-only candidates are marked UNVERIFIED regardless of how plausible their cited data sources are. Format per entry: description / posture / feasibility / size / impact 1-5 with evidence / merged-from.

## Judge's note on sequencing

Tier 1 items 1-4 share one substrate (the cached ITS_Errors/review-queue reads) and together answer the three questions every recent incident session opened with: is this real, is it new, and which rows do I resolve. Build them as one errors/review intelligence pass. In Tier 2, items 1-3 (parity badges, pollution filter, capacity gauges) are the three highest-recurrence savers on the board and all UNVERIFIED for the same shallow reason — nobody has confirmed the data is on the fetched rows; a half-day grounding spike on those three should precede any Tier-2 build order. T2-13 (watchdog results persistence) punches above its impact score: it is the named prerequisite for T2-9 and the clean path for T3-2 — schedule it early.
